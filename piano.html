What is a "Piano"?

A piano is a large, stringed musical instrument that produces sound by striking strings with hammers when keys on the keyboard are pressed. It is one of the most versatile and widely used instruments in both classical and modern music, found in a variety of genres including classical, jazz, pop, and rock.

Famous Piano Brands

Steinway & Sons

Steinway & Sons is a prestigious and highly respected piano manufacturer, renowned for producing some of the finest pianos in the world. Founded in 1853 by Heinrich Engelhard Steinweg (later anglicized to Henry Steinway) in New York City, Steinway & Sons has built a legacy of excellence in piano craftsmanship, innovation, and sound quality.

Bösendorfer

Bösendorfer is one of the most prestigious and renowned piano brands in the world, known for producing exceptional grand and upright pianos of the highest quality. Founded in 1828 by Ignaz Bösendorfer in Vienna, Austria, the company has a long history of crafting pianos that are celebrated for their rich tonal quality, powerful resonance, and intricate craftsmanship. Bösendorfer is particularly recognized for its dedication to perfection and its innovation in the field of piano design.

Sauter

Sauter is a prestigious German piano brand known for its exceptional craftsmanship, precision, and high-quality sound. Founded in 1819 by Johann Sauter in the town of Spaichingen, Germany, the company has a long history of producing pianos that blend traditional methods with modern innovations. Sauter pianos are widely regarded for their beautiful tonal quality, elegant design, and superior craftsmanship.
