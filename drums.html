What are "Drums"?

Drums are a type of percussion instrument that produce sound by striking or hitting a surface, typically a drumhead or membrane, with sticks, mallets, or hands. They are one of the oldest and most essential instruments in many musical traditions, ranging from classical orchestras to modern rock bands, jazz ensembles, and various cultural performances.

Famous Drums Brands

Pearl

Pearl Drums is one of the most well-known and respected drum brands in the world, recognized for its innovation, quality, and reliability. Founded in 1946 by Katsumi Yanagisawa in Japan, Pearl has become a global leader in the drum manufacturing industry. The brand has earned a reputation for producing drums that cater to a wide range of drummers, from beginners to professionals, and across various music genres, including rock, jazz, metal, and pop.

Gretsch

Gretsch Drums is a highly respected and iconic drum brand known for its rich history, craftsmanship, and distinctive sound. Founded in 1883 by Friedrich Gretsch in Brooklyn, New York, Gretsch has built a legacy of producing drums that are favored by musicians across many genres, including jazz, rock, blues, and pop. The brand is especially renowned for its high-quality drum kits, snare drums, and distinctive drum sounds.

Ludwig

Ludwig Drums is one of the most iconic and respected drum brands in the world, known for its high-quality craftsmanship and innovative designs. Founded in 1909 by William F. Ludwig in Chicago, Illinois, Ludwig quickly became a leader in the drum manufacturing industry and remains a top choice for drummers today. The brand is especially renowned for its drum kits, snare drums, and hardware, and has a long legacy of being used by legendary drummers across many musical genres, particularly in rock, jazz, and pop.
